Welcome To Explore The Beauty Of Skardu




SKARDU:


Skardu is a picturesque town in the Gilgit-Baltistan region of Pakistan. It is the capital of the Skardu District and is known for its stunning natural beauty. Skardu is often referred to as the gateway to some of the world's highest peaks, including K2, the second-highest mountain on Earth.



Location:


Skardu is located in the northeastern part of Pakistan, near the borders with China and India.situated in the heart of the Karakoram Range.


Scenic Beauty:


Skardu is renowned for its breathtaking landscapes, including snow-capped mountains, pristine lakes, and lush valleys. The Shangrila Resort, Upper Kachura Lake, Shigar Fort, and Satpara Lake are some of the popular tourist attractions in the area.


Trekking and Mountaineering:


The region around Skardu is a hub for trekking and mountaineering expeditions. Trekkers and climbers often use Skardu as a base to access the Karakoram Range, which boasts some of the world's most challenging peaks.


Culture:


Skardu is home to a diverse mix of cultures and ethnic groups, including Balti, Shina, and Kashmiri people. The local culture is influenced by Tibetan Buddhism and Islam.


Climate:


Skardu has a cold desert climate, with harsh winters and mild summers. It is a popular destination for tourists during the summer months when the weather is relatively pleasant.


Transport:


Skardu is accessible by road from Islamabad and Gilgit, but the road journey can be challenging due to rough terrain. It also has a small airport, Skardu Airport, with limited flight connections.
